Cannabis Sativa

Noah Gansallo

Cannabis Sativa has been cultivated throughout our history. The use that is later intended for the plant determines how it is cultivated. The use indicated by the graphic on the right, is recreational. The seeds from a Cannabis Sativa plants can be used to make hempseed oil, which is used for cooking, lamps and paints. As well as this, the seeds can be used as bird feed. The use that is most commonly known stems from the flowers of the Cannabis Sativa, known as marijuana which is used for recreational, medicinal and spiritual purposes.

The flowers of the Cannabis Sativa are arranged in racemes and can produce hundreds of seeds. The Cannabis Sativa plant is extremely sensitive. In its vegetative stage, the plant requires 12 - 13 hours of sunlight daily. If it does not receive this nutrients, the plant will stop producing seeds. The three main cultivar groups of Cannabis Sativa:

  • Growing for the fibre. This is the primary cultivar of Cannabis Sativa
  • Growing for the seeds, which can be eaten raw or for extracting hempseed oil
  • Growing for medicinal and recreational purposes
